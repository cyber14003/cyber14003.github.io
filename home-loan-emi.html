What is a Home Loan?

A home loan is a long-term loan provided by banks and financial institutions to help individuals purchase, construct, or renovate a residential property. The loan amount is repaid through monthly installments known as EMIs.

How Home Loan EMI is Calculated

Home loan EMI depends on three key factors: loan amount, interest rate, and loan tenure. EMIs are calculated using compound interest, where each installment includes both principal repayment and interest.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Can home loan EMIs change over time?
Yes, if you have a floating interest rate, EMIs may change depending on interest rate fluctuations.

2. Is longer tenure better for home loans?
Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest paid.

3. Can I prepay my home loan?
Most banks allow prepayment, which can reduce interest burden.

4. Does this calculator show exact bank EMIs?
The results are estimates and may vary slightly from bank calculations.
