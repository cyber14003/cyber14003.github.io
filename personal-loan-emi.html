What is a Personal Loan?

A personal loan is an unsecured loan offered by banks and financial institutions to meet personal financial needs such as medical expenses, travel, education, or debt consolidation. Since it does not require collateral, interest rates are usually higher than secured loans.

How Personal Loan EMI is Calculated

Personal loan EMIs are calculated based on the loan amount, interest rate, and repayment tenure. Each EMI includes a portion of the principal amount and interest, calculated using compound interest.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Are personal loan interest rates fixed?
Personal loan interest rates can be fixed or floating, depending on the lender.

2. Can I prepay my personal loan?
Yes, many lenders allow prepayment, though some may charge a prepayment fee.

3. Is personal loan EMI tax deductible?
Generally, personal loan EMIs are not tax-deductible unless used for specific purposes like home renovation.

4. Does this calculator show exact bank EMIs?
The results are indicative and may vary slightly from actual bank calculations.
