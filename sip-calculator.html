What is SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined way of investing in mutual funds where an investor invests a fixed amount at regular intervals, usually monthly. SIP allows investors to benefit from compounding and rupee cost averaging, making it one of the most preferred investment methods in India.

How Does SIP Work?

In SIP, a fixed amount is automatically invested in a selected mutual fund scheme on a predefined date. Over time, more units are purchased when markets are low and fewer units when markets are high. This helps reduce the impact of market volatility and builds long-term wealth.

Benefits of Investing Through SIP

  • Encourages disciplined and regular investing
  • Reduces market timing risk
  • Power of compounding over long term
  • Affordable investment option for all income groups
  • Suitable for long-term financial goals

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is SIP better than lump sum investment?
SIP is generally preferred for long-term investors as it reduces market timing risk and promotes disciplined investing.

2. Can I stop or modify my SIP?
Yes, SIPs are flexible and can be paused, modified, or stopped at any time depending on the mutual fund provider.

3. What is a good SIP duration?
A longer duration (10–20 years) usually provides better compounding benefits.

4. Are SIP returns guaranteed?
No, SIP returns depend on market performance. This calculator provides estimated results for educational purposes.

5. Is SIP suitable for beginners?
Yes, SIP is ideal for beginners due to its simplicity and low investment requirement.
